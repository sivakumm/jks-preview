Kadanthu Sella(vaa)

written & directed by Jenu K. Siva

A young nurse befriends a dementia patient called Saritha at the hospital, where she works. They get to know each other and the more time passes, the more they get closer. Saritha opens up about her past as the nurse questions her. Saritha narrates about her first love, her forced marriage and the torments she had to endure in her life.

Jenu K. Siva

Part-time filmmaker & full time student.

I have been telling stories ever since I learned to write. Something which started as an exercise to learn the language evolved into a passion.

I see filmmaking as a wonderful accumulation of various art forms like painting, writing, music and acting. To bring someone's vision from paper to screen is pure magic in my opinion. I believe that cinema is bigger than all of us. One can't put cinema in a box and define it as such.

Films can be entertaining, educating and empowering. It is a form of self-expression and helps one to step into the lives of different human beings, anims and even fantasy creatures. I personally navigate towards personal stories around me. The untold stories of my people and kin. I want to bring my and their experiences to the screens and yet experiment myself in all genres possible. Like Scorsese said, I want to study the old masters, enrich my palette and expand the canvas.
